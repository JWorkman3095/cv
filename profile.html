Life Before Bootcamp

Prior to attending MAX Technical Training Bootcamp I spent years working in retail warehousing, maunfacturing and the logistics industry. When the pandemic hit in 2020 I found myself unemployed and wondering what I was going to do next. I knew that years of being a singer-songwriter and touring to suppliment my income was also not going to provide the lifestyle I had grown acustom to, especially since that industry was also hit hard. I spoke with a now former band mate, who graduated in 2021, and he told me all about the great experience he had during his enrollment. When everyone started returning back to work I found myself in an environment that wasn't going to last long or provide me with any oppurtunities for advancment. I had never been more unhappy getting up everyday to go to a job than I was in 2021. That is when I made the decision to alter my course and take matters into my own hands. I called MAX!

Life During Bootcamp

Being a U.S. Army Veteran I figured since I had been through Boot Camp once already that I could tackel this new challange. I can honestly say that my first go around was tough, both mentally and physically. The C# / .NET MAXimum Coding Boot Camp is no cake walk! It's just as tough! It may very well be one of thee most challanging things I have ever done. All of my prior training aided in giving me the confidence that I could continue on those days that I was feeling overwhelmed and struggling.

Having folks on your side who want you to succeed makes a world of diffence. The instructors have held us acctountable and at the same time provided the information and support to reach our goals. There are days when it seemed too much. I didn't think I could read one more term or definition, but then you find the support and encouragment to drive on. Former students, collegues, staff members all say, "Take some time and step away, take a break, relax and then get back to it." Sometimes that is all it took. When I could not see the forest because of the trees a step away was all I needed to get back at it. The reward of seeing the results makes all the long hours worth it.
